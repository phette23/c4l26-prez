<p class="large-text">
I ultimately redid all our URIs. In order of number of terms, I used: AAT, Wikidata, ULAN, TGN, and Homosaurus.
</p>
<p class="large-text">
It is worth revisiting the ideological commitments of organizations that undergird our data and systems. Centralizing around terms controlled by any political entity comes with innate risk, much as dependence on corporate software can be dangerous. It is unreasonable to expect community-run efforts to replace the decades of work behind the Library of Congress vocabularies. Diversifying, instead of replacing, our sources is the most realistic approach.
</p>
